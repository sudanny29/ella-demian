Chronic Pain & Integrative Therapies

Course: NUR 168 Integrative Theories & Practices

This group presentation explored complementary and alternative medicine (CAM) approaches for chronic pain management. The project covered chronic pain pathophysiology, evidence-based CAM modalities including acupuncture, massage therapy, yoga, chiropractic care, and mindfulness/meditation, along with safety considerations, ethical and legal principles, and financial considerations.

Key topics included the importance of integrating CAM into nursing care plans, reducing opioid reliance through non-pharmacologic approaches, building therapeutic relationships by being open to patient CAM use, and supporting culturally sensitive, patient-centered care.

The presentation concluded with a case study applying CAM interventions to a patient with chronic low back pain, demonstrating practical clinical application of integrative therapies.

Preeclampsia: Diagnosis & Nursing Care

Institution: Mount Saint Mary's University

Comprehensive group project on preeclampsia covering the full clinical picture: pathophysiology of poor placental development leading to endothelial damage, diagnostic criteria (hypertension after 20 weeks with proteinuria or organ dysfunction), and differentiation from gestational and chronic hypertension.

The project included detailed coverage of risk factors, lab work interpretation (CBC, CMP, LDH, urine protein), fetal surveillance methods (NST, BPP, Doppler studies), and medical management including labetalol, hydralazine, nifedipine, and magnesium sulfate with calcium gluconate as antidote.

A complete nursing interventions section covered priorities such as seizure prevention, BP stabilization, fetal monitoring, and fluid balance management. The project culminated in a detailed SBAR communication scenario for a severe preeclampsia case with possible HELLP syndrome development.
